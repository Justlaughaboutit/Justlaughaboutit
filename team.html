Meet the Team

Lesego

Lesego Tevin Mthembu, born in 1994 in the heart of Soweto, is a creative soul shaped by community, culture, and rhythm. From a young age, he showed leadership and dedication, actively participating in school activities like scholar patrol, soccer, and gumboot dancing. His love for music runs deep he’s especially drawn to the powerful messages of reggae and the raw storytelling of hip-hop legends like Tupac Shakur and The Notorious B.I.G. For Lesego, music is more than just sound; it’s a source of inspiration, reflection, and connection to identity and purpose.

Sabelo

Hailing from the vibrant heart of Soweto, our host brings a unique blend of street smarts, humor, and heartfelt storytelling to every episode. With a natural gift for turning everyday moments into laugh-out-loud anecdotes, he’s the friend you didn’t know you needed in your earbuds. Growing up in a place rich with culture and resilience, his perspective is raw, real, and always relatable. Whether he's joking about daily struggles or reflecting on life’s deeper lessons, he’s here to help you laugh about it—because sometimes, that’s the best way to deal with it.

Thato

Thato Moleko is a creative force to be reckoned with, born 1995 in the vibrant streets of White City Jabavu, where he developed a passion for storytelling and the arts. He attended Panterosa Primary School and Tulip Secondary School, laying the foundation for his future pursuits. As a media enthusiast, Thato serves as an anchor for the Just Laugh About It podcast, where he leverages his passion for storytelling, presenting, music, and art. With a relentless drive and creative energy, Thato is fuelled by his passion for his craft, his enthusiasm for innovation, and a commitment to bringing fresh perspectives to his work.

Owethu

Owethu Biyela is a vibrant content creator from Mandlazini, a village in Richards Bay, whose work is deeply inspired by her roots. Growing up in a community with limited access to resources, she made it her mission to challenge traditional norms and offer a bold, fresh perspective on life. Owethu brings fearless energy, humor, and heart to the podcast always ready to speak on topics others shy away from, especially around tradition, women empowerment, and youth. With a background in performing arts and film, she’s no stranger to the spotlight, having worked on major productions like Shaka iLembe and Adulting. Her goal? To inspire courage in others to dream beyond limits and embrace their power to change the world.

Kamohelo

Kamohelo Khalefi, born and raised in White City Jabavu, Soweto, is the fourth of five siblings and a proud product of Morris Isaacson High School. Although naturally introverted and reserved, he brings thoughtful insights and humor to the podcast. What started as a favor to a friend turned into a passion, allowing him to share and reflect on life’s everyday struggles and lessons. A self-described “hustler,” Kamohelo connects deeply with township life and sees the podcast as a space to explore and grow beyond it. He values honest conversation, real experiences, and hopes listeners especially everyday hustlers take away meaningful lessons and laughter from each episode.
